## Summary

Defines the worst forms of child labour, including trafficking, slavery, forced recruitment, and commercial sexual exploitation of children (Art. 3). Requires States to take effective and time-bound measures for identifying and reaching out to children at special risk, which directly underpins child victim identification efforts.

## Key Provisions

Art. 3 (worst forms of child labour); Art. 7(2) (effective and time-bound measures for identification and outreach)
