## Summary

First EU criminal law instrument specifically targeting trafficking in human beings, establishing common definitions and requiring effective, proportionate, and dissuasive penalties. Superseded by Directive 2011/36/EU but remains historically significant as the foundation for harmonised EU anti-trafficking law and victim identification obligations.

## Key Provisions

Arts. 1–2 (offences), Art. 3 (penalties), Art. 7 (protection and assistance to victims)
