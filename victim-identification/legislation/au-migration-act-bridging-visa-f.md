## Summary

The Migration Act 1958 and associated Migration Regulations 1994 establish the Bridging Visa F (Class WF, Subclass 060) as the visa pathway for persons identified as victims of trafficking, slavery, or slavery-like practices. The visa enables identified victims who are unlawful non-citizens to remain lawfully in Australia while they access the Support for Trafficked People Program and, where applicable, assist with criminal investigations and prosecutions. This visa category is a critical interface between immigration enforcement and victim identification.

## Key Provisions

Migration Act 1958, s 73; Migration Regulations 1994, Schedule 1 Item 1306 (Class WF), Schedule 2 Subclass 060
