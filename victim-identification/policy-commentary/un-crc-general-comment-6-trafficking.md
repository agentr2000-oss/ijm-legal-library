## Summary

Authoritative interpretive guidance on the Convention on the Rights of the Child as applied to unaccompanied and separated children, including trafficking-related provisions addressing identification, guardianship, and non-refoulement. Highlights the particular vulnerability of unaccompanied children to trafficking and requires States to take targeted identification measures, including age-appropriate and gender-sensitive information campaigns.

## Key Provisions

Paras. 50–63 (trafficking-specific provisions); Paras. 31–38 (initial assessment and identification); Paras. 21–23 (guardianship and legal representation)
