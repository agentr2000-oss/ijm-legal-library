## Summary

Research study commissioned by ASEAN-ACT (successor to AAPTIP) examining victim identification practices and protection frameworks across seven ASEAN member states. Finds that most countries lack explicit victim identification provisions in their trafficking laws and that the absence of nationally endorsed, consistently applied identification guidelines results in differing interpretations. Recommends increasing screening focal points, especially in remote and border areas, and engaging NGOs in the identification process.
