## Summary

Reports and country-visit findings from the OSCE Special Representative identifying persistent policy gaps in victim identification across participating States, including lack of proactive outreach, inadequate identification of labour trafficking victims, and insufficient formal NRM structures. Recommends mapping risk areas, strengthening online-platform monitoring, and adopting social-pathway approaches to identification that operate independently of criminal-justice cooperation requirements.
