## Summary

Policy paper from the Anti-Trafficking Monitoring Group (convened by Anti-Slavery International) examining the UK's victim identification framework. Critiques the conflation of immigration enforcement with modern slavery identification, documents how increasing numbers of survivors have been barred from NRM access, and recommends that victim identification be treated as a protection rather than an immigration matter.

## Key Provisions

Sections on NRM access barriers, immigration-enforcement conflation, recommendations for consistent identification standards across UK jurisdictions
