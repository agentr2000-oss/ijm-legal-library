## Summary

Major parliamentary inquiry report tabled 18 July 2017 containing 20 recommendations for government action on human trafficking, slavery, and slavery-like practices in Australia. The inquiry examined the adequacy of the legislative framework, law enforcement response, victim support mechanisms, and supply chain transparency, contributing directly to the subsequent passage of the Modern Slavery Act 2018. The Australian Government response, coordinated by the Australian Border Force as chair of the Interdepartmental Committee on Human Trafficking and Slavery, was released in October 2020.
