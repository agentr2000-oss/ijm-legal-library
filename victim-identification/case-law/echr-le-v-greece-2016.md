summary: >
  The Court found that Greece violated Article 4 due to an unjustified delay in formally
  identifying the applicant, a Nigerian woman, as a trafficking victim despite her repeated
  reports to police. The judgment established that states must act promptly upon receiving
  credible indicators of trafficking and that delays in formal identification can themselves
  constitute a breach of the positive obligation under Art. 4.
