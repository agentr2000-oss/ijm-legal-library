summary: >
  Leading High Court authority on the definition of slavery under Division 270 of the Criminal Code. The Court held, by a 6-1 majority, that slavery extends beyond chattel slavery to encompass de facto conditions where powers attaching to the right of ownership are exercised over a person. The prosecution need only establish the accused's intention to exercise such powers, not knowledge of the source of those powers. This decision is foundational for victim identification, as it confirms that indicators of control and exploitation — not formal legal ownership — define slavery in Australian law.
