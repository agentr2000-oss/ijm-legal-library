  Landmark ECHR judgment establishing that Article 4 (prohibition of slavery/forced labour)
  imposes positive obligations on states to identify potential trafficking victims. The Court
  held that states must have an adequate legal and administrative framework for victim
  identification and must take operational measures to protect individuals where authorities
  are aware or ought to be aware of credible trafficking indicators.
