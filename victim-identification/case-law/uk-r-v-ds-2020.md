  Court of Appeal examined the application of the s. 45 Modern Slavery Act defence and
  the relationship between victim identification and non-punishment. Held that early
  identification of trafficking status is of paramount importance, and that a credible
  suspicion of trafficking must trigger a qualified assessment before any prosecution
  decision. The jury, not the judge on abuse of process, determines trafficking facts.
