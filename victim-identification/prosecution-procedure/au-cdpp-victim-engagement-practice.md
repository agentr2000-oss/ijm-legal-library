## Summary

The CDPP Victims of Crime Policy and associated Witness Assistance Service practice direction establish the framework for victim engagement throughout trafficking and slavery prosecutions. The policy requires CDPP staff to treat victims with courtesy, compassion, cultural sensitivity, and respect for their dignity, and mandates continuity of CDPP staff for vulnerable victims. All victims of slavery and sexual servitude offences must be referred to the Witness Assistance Service under a National Legal Direction, ensuring appropriate information and trauma-informed support throughout the prosecution process.
